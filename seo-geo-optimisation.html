SEO vs GEO

What is the difference between
SEO and GEO?

Old

Optimise for keywords only

Traditional SEO focuses on keyword density and placement to rank in Google's blue links.

New

Optimise for extractable answers

GEO requires direct, factual answers in the first 100 words that AI systems can cite verbatim.

Old

Build backlinks for authority

Backlinks had a 0.218 correlation with AI visibility. They still matter for Google, but less for AI.

New

Build brand mentions for citation

Brand web mentions have a 0.664 correlation with AI visibility. Mentions on YouTube score 0.737.

Old

Rely on meta tags and sitemaps

Google reads your sitemap.xml, but AI crawlers need additional structured guidance.

New

Deploy llms.txt and structured data

AI crawlers read llms.txt to understand your site. JSON-LD schema helps them extract facts.

Why you need both, not one or the other

SEO and GEO are not competing strategies. They are complementary layers of the same visibility system. Google still drives the majority of web traffic today. But AI-powered search is growing at a pace that makes ignoring it genuinely risky.

The good news: most GEO best practices improve your traditional SEO as well. Answer capsules reduce bounce rates. Structured data earns rich snippets. Question-based headings capture featured snippets. You do not have to choose. You build both at once.

The key difference for regulated industries: compliance adds a layer of complexity. Answer capsules must be factual without making prohibited claims. Structured data must accurately represent services without breaching AHPRA, ASIC, or TGA advertising rules. That is where specialist knowledge matters.

I have spent years writing for healthcare, fintech, legal, and NDIS providers under Australian regulatory frameworks. I know which claims trigger reviews and which structures keep you safe. Your compliance team will thank you.
