My story

I didn't start with AI. I started with 200 projects that taught me what actually ranks.

Let me save you a conversation. Yes, I use AI. Every day. Claude, ChatGPT, the works. And no, that's not the interesting part.

The interesting part is the five years before AI was cool. The part where I was obsessively studying what makes a page rank on Google, what makes a visitor click "book now" instead of bouncing, and why most content strategies look great in a deck and produce absolutely nothing in real life.

200+ projects across fintech, healthcare, SaaS, ecommerce, and D2C. Three continents. Landing pages that tripled conversion rates. Blog strategies that drove six figures in organic traffic. Email sequences with open rates that made marketing directors do a double-take.

"AI made me faster. But the five years before it? That's what makes me dangerous."

Today I work at CJ&CO, one of Australia's top digital agencies. I write and optimize content for healthcare brands under AHPRA, fintech companies under ASIC, and therapeutic goods businesses under TGA codes. These are industries where ChatGPT will confidently write a claim that gets your ad pulled, your listing flagged, or your practice reported. I catch those before they go live. Because I've seen what happens when someone doesn't.

Here's what most people miss about AI and content. The writing part was never the hard part. The hard part is knowing which keyword triggers a Google healthcare policy review. Which claim structure survives AHPRA scrutiny. Which page architecture makes Google and ChatGPT both want to cite you. That stuff doesn't come from a prompt. It comes from years of watching what works, what tanks, and what gets a cease-and-desist.

I'm ISB-trained (Indian School of Business, top-ranked in Asia). Generative AI certified. And I apply GEO to every project, because Google is no longer the only search engine that matters. When someone asks Perplexity or ChatGPT "who's the best physiotherapist in Melbourne" or "which fintech app has the lowest fees," your content either shows up or it doesn't. I make sure it does.

Based in Delhi. Serving brands across Australia, the US, and the UK. My timezone means your content gets built while you sleep. My track record means you skip the part where you pay someone to learn your industry.

What I believe

The stuff nobody says out loud.

01

AI didn't kill writing. Bad strategy did. I use Claude and ChatGPT every single day. They're incredible tools. But a tool without direction produces noise. The direction, the judgment, the "this will get your ad pulled" instinct? That's the actual job.

02

Most "compliant" content is just boring content with a disclaimer. In regulated industries, brands water down their messaging until it says nothing. That's not compliance. That's surrender. Real skill is writing something that hits hard and still passes regulatory review.

03

If you're only ranking on Google, you're already behind. Your next customer might ask Perplexity before they ever type a search. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) isn't a nice-to-have anymore. It's where your competitors are headed. I'm already there.

04

One blog post is a lottery ticket. A content system is a pipeline. Posting and praying is not a strategy. A system that maps keywords to buyer stages, structures content for AI citation, and compounds over time? That's what actually moves revenue.

05

Depth wins. Volume is just expensive noise. One page that genuinely answers the question your customer is asking will outperform fifty AI-generated filler posts. Google knows it. ChatGPT knows it. Your bounce rate proves it.
