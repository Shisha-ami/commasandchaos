My philosophy

What I believe
about content.

Good content isn't just well-written. It's well-positioned. It knows who it's for, what it needs to do, and how to make someone care enough to keep reading.

I work with brands that are tired of content that fills a calendar but doesn't move the needle. The brands that want copy their customers actually want to read, and SEO that brings in people who buy. Not just people who bounce.

  • Every sentence earns the next one. If it doesn't earn its place, it doesn't belong on the page. (This applies to every word in your Google ad too.)
  • Brand voice is a product decision. Not a style guide footnote. When everything sounds consistent, customers trust you more. Full stop.
  • SEO and good writing are not in conflict. They have never been in conflict. If your SEO content reads like SEO content, the brief was wrong.
  • Depth beats volume. One page that genuinely helps your audience converts better than ten that don't. Every single time.
  • The best copy solves the reader's problem before they finish asking the question. That's the goal.
