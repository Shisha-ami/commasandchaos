The Problem

Running Google Ads in a regulated industry is a different game. Here is what goes wrong.

01

Your ads get disapproved, again and again

Google's healthcare and financial services ad policies are strict and constantly updated. Your ads get flagged for restricted content, unapproved claims, or missing certifications. Each disapproval costs you days of lost visibility while competitors keep running. Resubmissions pile up. Your account health score drops.

02

Your ads might be running, but the copy is generic. "Trusted professionals. Quality service. Contact us today." Nothing differentiates you. Click-through rates are low, cost per click is high, and the leads that do come through are unqualified. You are paying for impressions that do nothing.

03

Your landing pages break the conversion chain

The ad promises one thing. The landing page says something else. Message mismatch kills conversions and tanks your Quality Score. In regulated industries, this problem is worse because you cannot just write anything on the landing page either. Compliance requirements apply to both the ad and the destination.

04

Your Quality Score is tanking because of your copy

Google rewards ads with high relevance, strong CTR, and good landing page experience. Bad copy drags all three metrics down. You end up paying more per click than competitors with the same bid, because your Quality Score penalises you at every auction. The gap compounds over time.

Ad Policy Compliance

What Google allows and disallows
in regulated ad categories.

✕

No guarantees of outcomes or results

"Guaranteed approval" or "100% success rate" will get your ad disapproved instantly in healthcare and finance.

✕

No misleading claims about credentials

Implying government endorsement, fake certifications, or inflated qualifications triggers immediate policy violations.

✕

No restricted drug or treatment terms

Prescription medication names, unapproved treatment claims, and certain medical procedure terms are restricted or blocked entirely.

✓

Educational and informational framing works

"Learn about your options" and "understand the process" are compliant ways to drive clicks without triggering policy flags.

✓

Credentials and trust signals are encouraged

Mentioning real qualifications, registrations (AHPRA, AFSL, Law Society), and years of experience builds trust and passes review.

✓

Process-focused CTAs convert well

"Book a consultation," "request a review," and "speak with an adviser" are compliant, specific, and high-converting CTAs.
