Conversion Architecture

Weak copy patterns vs. copy
that actually converts.

✕

"We provide holistic solutions for your needs"

Generic. Says nothing about what you do, for whom, or why it matters. Every competitor says the same thing.

✓

"NDIS plan management for 2,400+ participants across NSW"

Specific service, specific proof point, specific geography. A visitor knows in 3 seconds if this is for them.

✕

"Contact us to learn more"

Weak CTA with no value proposition. Why would someone contact you? What do they get?

✓

"Book a free 15-minute plan review. No commitment."

Clear action, clear value, clear risk reduction. The visitor knows exactly what happens next.

✕

"Our team of experienced professionals"

Empty credential claim. No specifics. No proof. Every business says this. None of them should.

✓

"12 registered nurses. 8 years average experience. AHPRA-compliant since 2016."

Numbers, credentials, and regulatory standing. Trust built through evidence, not adjectives.

The framework behind every page I write

Conversion is not about clever words. It is about architecture. Every page I write follows a four-layer structure that moves a visitor from awareness to action.

Layer 1: Messaging hierarchy. The most important information appears first. Your headline answers "what is this and is it for me?" in under 5 seconds. Supporting copy builds the case in order of priority, not in the order you thought of it.

Layer 2: Trust signals. Credentials, client counts, regulatory standing, and proof points placed at friction points. Not buried on an about page. Placed exactly where doubt creeps in.

Layer 3: CTA architecture. One primary action per page. Secondary actions that do not compete. Micro-commitments that reduce perceived risk. Every CTA answers the question: "What do I get and what does it cost me?"

Layer 4: Compliance checkpoints. For regulated industries, every claim is checked against the relevant framework: AHPRA for healthcare, ASIC for financial services, TGA for therapeutic goods, ACL for consumer guarantees. Your legal team reviews clean copy, not a minefield.
